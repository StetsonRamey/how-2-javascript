Who am I?

I am Stetson. This site is a public record of my attemps to understand the technological world, and to speak it's language.

My history of learning to program is scattered and long. I started with Ruby, but didn't get far, like maybe 2 weeks far. Then I dabbled with Wordpress/PHP, but didn't stick with it. Then plain old HTML and CSS which I learned well enough to build a few sites.

In 2018 I did a coding bootcamp focused on the MERN stack (not nearly as cool sounding as the MEAN stack in my opinion) and that was my first journey into Javascript.

Between programming stints I started two compaines. Great Plains Painting & Tis the Season Holiday Lighting.

In 2019 I sold my painting company and 2020 was the year I got serious about learning Javascript and doing something with it.

What I'll Write About Here

I've read A LOT of material on learning to code. I've watched A LOT of videos and in my opinion most "educators" assume that they know what you need to learn.

I'm going to try something different. I'm going to tell you what worked for me and why I went about it that way. I'll make 0 assumptions about it being the way you should do it.

Note: I'm going to be wrong a lot. I know slightly more than nothing and am far from an expert. Hopefully reading about me struggling through figuring things out is helpful for you.
